The phrase "Mind the gap" has its origins in the United Kingdom in 1968 as a visual and audio cue warning passangers boarding trains to watch the gap between the platform and the train.

Our game, "Mind the gap" is a "fill in the gap" semantic game, requiring quick thinking, a large vocabulary and the ability to make word connections and fill in letter gaps correctly.

On entering a word (e.g. baby), a definition (e.g. a nutritional substance produced by mammals) of a related word (e.g. milk) is provided. Your task is to guess the correct related word. A correct word will earn you more time!, while an incorrect letter entered into "the gap" will simply result in an incorrect word response.

Use all provided tools: definitions, hints and your innate guessing ability to correctly guess 10 words and color the game phrase "Mind the Gap," all BEFORE the Timer runs out! Remember above all...
